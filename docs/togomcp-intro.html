TogoMCP

A comprehensive Model Context Protocol server for life sciences research, providing LLM agents with seamless access to 20+ integrated biological databases through SPARQL, RDF exploration, and ID conversion services.

Endpoint: https://togomcp.rdfportal.org/mcp
Overview Databases Tools Setup Examples Resources

Overview

TogoMCP is a comprehensive Model Context Protocol (MCP) server that bridges the gap between AI language models and the vast ecosystem of life sciences databases. Developed by the Database Center for Life Science (DBCLS), TogoMCP empowers researchers to seamlessly query, explore, and integrate data across more than 20 major biological and biomedical databases.

üéØ What TogoMCP Offers

For Biologists and Medical Researchers: Ask questions in natural language and get integrated answers from multiple databases without needing to know SPARQL or database-specific query languages.

For Bioinformaticians: Leverage powerful SPARQL endpoints, explore RDF data structures with MIE (Metadata Interoperability Exchange) files, and perform sophisticated cross-database queries using standardized semantic web technologies.

Available Databases

TogoMCP provides access to a comprehensive collection of biological and biomedical databases spanning proteins, genes, chemical compounds, diseases, pathways, and more. All databases are maintained with regular updates and accessible through standardized RDF/SPARQL endpoints.

UniProt
UniProt Protein Knowledge Base
Comprehensive protein sequence and functional information integrating Swiss-Prot (manually curated, 923K entries) and TrEMBL (automatically annotated, 444M entries). Contains sequences, functions, domains, structures, variants, and cross-references to 200+ databases.
PubMed
PubMed Biomedical Literature Database
Bibliographic information for biomedical literature from MEDLINE, life science journals, and online books. Includes publication metadata, abstracts, authors, affiliations, MeSH term annotations, and cross-references to external databases.
ChEMBL
ChEMBL Bioactivity Database
Manually curated database of bioactive molecules with 2.4M+ compounds, 1.6M assays, 20M bioactivity measurements. Enables queries for compound-target-activity relationships, mechanism of action, and drug repositioning.
PubChem
PubChem Chemical Database
Comprehensive public database with 119M compounds, 339M substances, 1.7M bioassays, 167K genes, 249K proteins, and 81K pathways. Central hub linking compounds to genes, proteins, pathways, diseases, and literature.
ChEBI
Chemical Entities of Biological Interest
Ontology database containing 217,000+ chemical entities including small molecules, atoms, ions, and functional groups with hierarchical classification, molecular data, biological roles, and cross-references to 20+ databases.
Reactome
Reactome Pathway Database
Open-source, curated knowledgebase of biological pathways with 22,000+ pathways across 30+ species, including molecular interactions, biochemical reactions, protein complexes, and disease associations based on BioPAX Level 3 ontology.
Rhea
Rhea Biochemical Reactions Database
Expert-curated database of 17,078 biochemical reactions (34,156 directional representations). All reactions are atom-balanced, chemically annotated, and linked to ChEBI, KEGG, MetaCyc, Reactome, and Gene Ontology.
PDB
Protein Data Bank
3D structural data for proteins, nucleic acids, and complexes from X-ray, NMR, and cryo-EM with 204K+ entries. Contains experimental methods, resolution, sequences, and cross-references to UniProt, EMDB, and GenBank.
Ensembl
Ensembl Genome Database
Comprehensive genomics database with annotations for 100+ species. Contains genes, transcripts, proteins, and exons with genomic locations, cross-referenced to UniProt, HGNC, NCBI Gene, Reactome, and OMIM.
NCBI Gene
NCBI Gene Database
Gene database with 57M+ entries covering protein-coding genes, ncRNAs, and pseudogenes across all organisms. Includes symbols, descriptions, chromosomal locations, types, synonyms, and cross-references to Ensembl, HGNC, OMIM.
Gene Ontology (GO)
Gene Ontology
Controlled vocabulary for describing gene and gene product attributes with three ontology domains: biological_process (30,804 terms), molecular_function (12,793 terms), and cellular_component (4,568 terms).
MeSH
Medical Subject Headings
National Library of Medicine's controlled vocabulary thesaurus for biomedical literature indexing with hierarchical structure across 16 main categories including descriptors, qualifiers, and supplementary records.
MONDO
Monarch Disease Ontology
Comprehensive disease ontology integrating multiple disease databases into unified classification with cross-references to OMIM, Orphanet, DOID, MESH, ICD, and 35+ databases for clinical research and precision medicine.
ClinVar
ClinVar Genomic Variation Database
Aggregates genomic variation and relationships to human health with 3.5M+ variant records, clinical interpretations, gene associations, and disease conditions cross-referenced to MedGen, OMIM, MeSH, and HGNC.
NCBI Taxonomy
NCBI Taxonomy Database
Comprehensive biological taxonomic classification covering 3M+ organisms from bacteria to mammals with hierarchical relationships, scientific/common names, and genetic code assignments.
PubTator
PubTator Central Text Mining
Biomedical entity annotations extracted from PubMed literature using advanced text mining. Contains disease and gene annotations linked to articles using MeSH and NCBI Gene identifiers.
MedGen
Medical Genetics Portal
NCBI's portal for medical conditions with genetic components containing 233,000+ clinical concepts covering diseases, phenotypes, and clinical findings with data from OMIM, Orphanet, HPO, and MONDO.
NANDO
Nanbyo Data (Japanese Intractable Diseases)
Comprehensive ontology for Japanese intractable rare diseases with 2,777 disease classes. Includes multilingual labels, descriptions, notification numbers, and cross-references to MONDO and KEGG.
BacDive
Bacterial Diversity Metadatabase
Standardized bacterial and archaeal strain information with 97,000+ strain records covering taxonomy, morphology, physiology, cultivation conditions, molecular data, and cross-references to culture collections.
MediaDive
Microbial Culture Media Database
Comprehensive culture media database from DSMZ with 3,289 standardized recipes, 1,489 ingredients with chemical cross-references, 45,685 strain records, and detailed growth conditions.
DDBJ
DNA Data Bank of Japan
Nucleotide sequence data from INSDC with genomic annotations (genes, CDS, tRNA, rRNA) linked to organism metadata, taxonomic classification, functional annotations, and protein translations.
GlyCosmos
GlyCosmos Glycoscience Portal
Comprehensive glycoscience portal integrating glycan structures (GlyTouCan), glycoproteins, glycosylation sites, glycogenes, glycoepitopes, and lectin-glycan interactions for multi-species glycobiology research.

Available Tools

TogoMCP provides a rich set of tools for exploring and querying life sciences databases. These tools enable both direct SPARQL querying and high-level database operations.

Core SPARQL Tools

list_databases
List all available RDF databases with detailed descriptions of their contents, scope, and key entities.
get_MIE_file
Retrieve MIE (Metadata Interoperability Exchange) files containing ShEx schemas, RDF examples, and SPARQL templates for understanding database structure before querying.
run_sparql
Execute SPARQL queries on specific RDF databases. Supports SELECT, CONSTRUCT, and ASK queries with full SPARQL 1.1 features.
get_sparql_endpoints
Get the list of available SPARQL endpoints for direct database access.
get_sparql_example
Retrieve example SPARQL queries for a specific database to learn query patterns and best practices.
get_graph_list
List named graphs available in a specific RDF database for targeted querying.

Database-Specific Search Tools

search_uniprot_entity
Search for UniProt protein entities by query. Returns protein IDs with matching names or descriptions.
search_chembl_molecule
Search ChEMBL for molecules/compounds by name or synonym.
search_chembl_target
Search ChEMBL for drug targets (proteins, cell lines, organisms).
search_chembl_id_lookup
General ChEMBL ID lookup across multiple entity types.
search_pdb_entity
Search PDB for protein structures, chemical components, or biologically interesting molecules by keywords.
search_mesh_entity
Search MeSH (Medical Subject Headings) for medical terms, diseases, and concepts.
get_pubchem_compound_id
Get PubChem compound ID by compound name.
get_compound_attributes_from_pubchem
Retrieve detailed attributes for a PubChem compound including molecular properties, structure, and classifications.

ID Conversion Tools (TogoID Integration)

togoid_getAllDataset
Get configuration for all available datasets in TogoID including labels, regex patterns, prefixes, and examples.
togoid_getDataset
Get detailed configuration for a specific dataset.
togoid_getAllRelation
Get all possible conversion relationships between databases.
togoid_getRelation
Get relationship details between two specific databases.
togoid_convertId
Convert IDs from one database to another, supporting multi-step conversions through intermediate databases.
togoid_countId
Count how many IDs can be converted between databases to assess coverage before performing full conversion.
togoid_getDescription
Get descriptions for all databases in multiple languages.

Guidance Tool

RDF_Portal_Guide
Comprehensive guide for using the RDF Portal effectively. Provides best practices for SPARQL querying, MIE file usage, and efficient cross-database data retrieval strategies.

Setup Guide

TogoMCP is provided via Streamable-HTTP transport, making it compatible with multiple AI platforms. Follow the instructions below for your preferred platform.

Setting up TogoMCP with Claude Desktop

Claude Desktop supports remote MCP servers through Custom Connectors. This feature is available for Pro, Max, Team, and Enterprise plans.

Step 1: Access Settings

  1. Open Claude Desktop or navigate to claude.ai
  2. Click on your profile icon and select Settings
  3. Navigate to the Connectors section

Step 2: Add Custom Connector

  1. Scroll to the bottom of the Connectors section
  2. Click "Add custom connector"
  3. In the dialog, enter the TogoMCP server URL:
https://togomcp.rdfportal.org/mcp

Step 3: Complete Setup

  1. Click "Add" to complete the configuration
  2. The connector will appear in your Connectors list

Step 4: Use the Connector

  1. In a chat, click the "Search and tools" button (below the prompt)
  2. Select the TogoMCP connector to enable it for your conversation
  3. Start asking questions about biological databases!
Note: For Team and Enterprise plans, an Owner must first enable the connector for the organization before individual users can authenticate and use it.

Setting up TogoMCP with ChatGPT

ChatGPT supports MCP connectors through Developer Mode, available for Pro, Plus, Business, Enterprise, and Education plans.

Step 1: Enable Developer Mode

  1. Open ChatGPT at chatgpt.com
  2. Navigate to Settings ‚Üí Connectors ‚Üí Advanced
  3. Toggle on "Developer Mode"
For Enterprise/Business: Workspace admins must first enable Developer Mode in Workspace Settings ‚Üí Permissions & Roles ‚Üí Connected Data Developer mode.

Step 2: Add MCP Connector

  1. In the Connectors tab, click "Create"
  2. Fill in the following details:
    • Name: TogoMCP
    • Description: Life sciences database integration
    • MCP server URL: https://togomcp.rdfportal.org/mcp
    • Authentication: None (leave as default)
  3. Click "Create"

Step 3: Use in Conversations

  1. Start a new chat
  2. Click the "+" icon in the composer
  3. Select "Developer Mode"
  4. Choose the TogoMCP connector as a source
  5. Begin querying biological databases!
Important: Write actions from MCP tools will require manual confirmation unless you choose to remember approvals for the conversation. Only approve actions from servers you trust.

Setting up TogoMCP with Gemini CLI

Gemini CLI uses a configuration file to connect to MCP servers. The setup process involves editing your settings.json file.

Step 1: Locate Settings File

Find your Gemini CLI settings file at:

  • macOS/Linux: ~/.gemini/settings.json
  • Windows: %USERPROFILE%\.gemini\settings.json

If the file doesn't exist, create it with an empty JSON object: {}

Step 2: Add MCP Server Configuration

Add the following configuration to your settings.json file:

{ "mcpServers": { "togomcp": { "httpUrl": "https://togomcp.rdfportal.org/mcp" } } }
Note: If you already have other MCP servers configured, add the "togomcp" entry to your existing "mcpServers" object.

Step 3: Restart Gemini CLI

After saving the configuration file, restart Gemini CLI to load the new server.

Step 4: Verify Connection

  1. Start Gemini CLI
  2. Use the /mcp command to verify the TogoMCP server is connected
  3. You should see TogoMCP listed with its available tools

Step 5: Use TogoMCP Tools

TogoMCP tools are now available to the Gemini model. Simply ask questions about biological databases, and Gemini will automatically use the appropriate TogoMCP tools.

Example: "Search UniProt for p53 protein and show me its cross-references to other databases."

Usage Examples

Here are practical examples demonstrating TogoMCP's capabilities for common biological research tasks:

Example 1: Finding Protein-Drug Interactions
Prompt: "Find drugs that target the EGFR protein and show their bioactivity data"
TogoMCP will:
  1. Search UniProt for EGFR protein (P00533)
  2. Use the UniProt ID to query ChEMBL for compounds targeting this protein
  3. Return bioactivity data including IC50 values, assay types, and compound structures
  4. Cross-reference compounds to PubChem for additional chemical properties

This query integrates data from UniProt, ChEMBL, and PubChem, providing a comprehensive view of EGFR inhibitors and their activity profiles.

Example 2: Literature Mining for Disease-Gene Associations
Prompt: "Find recent PubMed articles about Alzheimer's disease that mention APOE gene, and show me the gene's protein structure"
TogoMCP will:
  1. Search PubMed using MeSH terms for Alzheimer's disease
  2. Filter articles mentioning APOE using PubTator annotations
  3. Retrieve APOE gene information from NCBI Gene
  4. Find APOE protein structures in PDB
  5. Link to relevant clinical variants in ClinVar

This integrates literature, gene, protein, and clinical variant data to provide comprehensive insights into APOE's role in Alzheimer's disease.

Example 3: Metabolic Pathway Analysis
Prompt: "Show me the glycolysis pathway in humans and list the enzymes involved with their structures"
TogoMCP will:
  1. Query Reactome for the glycolysis pathway
  2. Extract enzyme information from the pathway
  3. Convert enzyme IDs to UniProt IDs using TogoID
  4. Query PDB for 3D structures of the enzymes
  5. Show biochemical reactions from Rhea database

This creates a comprehensive view of glycolysis combining pathway topology, enzyme information, 3D structures, and detailed biochemical reactions.

Example 4: Chemical Compound Property Analysis
Prompt: "Find information about resveratrol including its chemical properties, biological activities, and related compounds"
TogoMCP will:
  1. Get PubChem compound ID for resveratrol
  2. Retrieve molecular properties (SMILES, InChI, molecular weight, logP)
  3. Query ChEBI for chemical classification and biological roles
  4. Search ChEMBL for bioactivity data against various targets
  5. Find structurally similar compounds in PubChem

This provides a complete chemical and biological profile of resveratrol, useful for drug discovery and natural product research.

Example 5: Genetic Variant Clinical Significance
Prompt: "Find clinical significance of BRCA1 variants and associated diseases"
TogoMCP will:
  1. Search NCBI Gene for BRCA1
  2. Query ClinVar for BRCA1 variants with clinical interpretations
  3. Link variants to associated diseases via MedGen
  4. Cross-reference with MONDO for disease classification
  5. Find relevant literature in PubMed

This integrates genetic variant data with clinical interpretations and disease associations, essential for clinical genomics and precision medicine.
