What is TogoMCP?

TogoMCP is a comprehensive Model Context Protocol (MCP) server that provides AI assistants with seamless access to a vast ecosystem of life sciences databases through SPARQL queries, RDF data exploration, and ID conversion services. Developed by the Database Center for Life Science (DBCLS), TogoMCP integrates over 20 major biological and biomedical databases, offering researchers a powerful toolkit for cross-database queries, data integration, and knowledge discovery.

Why TogoMCP for Researchers?

TogoMCP bridges the gap between AI assistants and scientific databases, enabling researchers in biology and medicine to:

  • Ask natural language questions and receive data-driven answers from authoritative sources
  • Perform complex cross-database queries without learning SPARQL syntax
  • Convert identifiers seamlessly across different database systems
  • Integrate heterogeneous data from proteins, genes, compounds, diseases, and more
  • Accelerate hypothesis generation and literature discovery

Usage Examples

Here are real-world examples demonstrating TogoMCP's capabilities in biomedical research:

Example 1

Prompt: Find proteins that are associated with both cardiovascular diseases and have known small molecule inhibitors in ChEMBL, and classify them according to disease and drug availability.

Response:

TogoMCP orchestrated a multi-database integration strategy to identify and classify cardiovascular disease-associated proteins with small molecule inhibitors:

Tools Used:

  • list_databases - Surveyed available databases to plan the analysis strategy
  • search_mesh_entity - Located MeSH terms for cardiovascular diseases (D002318)
  • search_chembl_target - Searched for cardiovascular-related drug targets across multiple queries (hypertension, heart failure, arrhythmia, coronary artery disease)
  • get_MIE_file - Retrieved ChEMBL database schema and SPARQL query examples
  • run_sparql - Executed multiple SPARQL queries on ChEMBL database to extract:
    • Protein targets with associated inhibitors
    • Drug molecules and their bioactivity data
    • Development status and approval information
    • Disease associations and mechanism of action

Key Results:

  • Identified 75+ human proteins associated with cardiovascular diseases with known small molecule inhibitors
  • By disease category: Hypertension (15+ validated targets), Coronary Artery Disease (20+ targets), Heart Failure (12+ targets), Cardiac Arrhythmias (10+ ion channel blockers)
  • Most productive target families: Sodium Channels (57 inhibitors), L-Type Calcium Channels (27 inhibitors), Beta-1 Adrenergic Receptor (25+ inhibitors)
  • Drug development status: 50+ targets with approved drugs, 15+ targets in Phase 3 clinical trials

The analysis integrated data from ChEMBL (bioactivity), UniProt (protein data), and MeSH (disease classification), delivering comprehensive reports with detailed classifications, CSV summaries, and visual charts.

Example 2

Prompt: Determine the distribution of human enzymes by their EC number classes.

Response:

TogoMCP executed a systematic query strategy to analyze human enzyme distribution across EC classification system:

Tools Used:

  • get_MIE_file - Retrieved UniProt database schema to understand enzyme classification structure
  • run_sparql - Executed multiple SPARQL queries on UniProt database:
    • Retrieved all human enzymes with EC numbers from Swiss-Prot (reviewed entries)
    • Counted enzymes by EC class (EC 1-7)
    • Extracted specific examples for each enzyme class

Key Results:

Analyzed 4,442 unique human enzymes from UniProt's Swiss-Prot database with the following distribution:

  • EC 2 - Transferases: 1,823 proteins (41.0%) - Most abundant
  • EC 3 - Hydrolases: 1,655 proteins (37.3%) - Second most abundant
  • EC 1 - Oxidoreductases: 545 proteins (12.3%)
  • EC 4 - Lyases: 151 proteins (3.4%)
  • EC 5 - Isomerases: 148 proteins (3.3%)
  • EC 6 - Ligases: 124 proteins (2.8%)
  • EC 7 - Translocases: 103 proteins (2.3%)

Insights: Transferases and hydrolases dominate (78.3% combined), reflecting the critical importance of cellular signaling mechanisms (kinases, phosphatases) and regulatory processes (proteases, lipases) in human biology.

Example 3

Prompt: Use ChEBI (chemical classification) + ChEMBL (bioactivity) + PubChem (chemical descriptors) to compare the bioactivity profiles and chemical diversity of natural products versus synthetic compounds across different therapeutic areas.

Response:

TogoMCP conducted a comprehensive cross-database comparative analysis integrating chemical classification, bioactivity data, and molecular descriptors:

Tools Used:

  • get_MIE_file - Retrieved database schemas for ChEBI, ChEMBL, and PubChem to understand data structures
  • run_sparql - Executed SPARQL queries across multiple databases:
    • ChEBI: Identified natural products and their classifications
    • ChEMBL: Extracted bioactivity data, target information, and therapeutic classifications
    • Queried for specific natural products (vinblastine, paclitaxel, doxorubicin) and synthetic drugs (imatinib, omeprazole)
  • get_pubchem_compound_id - Retrieved PubChem IDs for specific compounds
  • get_compound_attributes_from_pubchem - Obtained molecular descriptors and chemical properties from PubChem

Key Results:

Natural Products characteristics:

  • Ultra-high potency: 70% show IC50 < 100 nM, with many sub-nanomolar
  • Chemical complexity: Average 8 chiral centers, MW 400-900 Da, higher sp3 fraction (0.6)
  • Polypharmacology: Hit 8.5 targets on average (multi-target action)
  • Therapeutic dominance: ~60% of anticancer drugs are natural product-derived

Synthetic Compounds characteristics:

  • Selective potency: 40% show IC50 < 100 nM, highly target-specific
  • Simpler structures: Average 1 chiral center, MW 300-600 Da, lower sp3 fraction (0.3)
  • Monopharmacology: Hit 2-3 targets (designed selectivity)
  • Broader coverage: Dominant in cardiovascular, CNS, metabolic diseases

Insights: Natural products excel in oncology by occupying unique chemical space with pre-validated biological activity from evolution. Synthetic drugs excel in target selectivity through rational design. The optimal drug discovery strategy combines both approaches: use natural products for novel scaffold discovery, then apply medicinal chemistry optimization for enhanced selectivity and pharmacological properties.

Setup Guide

TogoMCP can be connected to your favorite AI assistant through the Model Context Protocol. Follow the setup instructions for your preferred platform:

Claude Desktop

Claude Desktop (Pro, Max, Team, and Enterprise plans) supports custom connectors for remote MCP servers through a user-friendly interface.

Navigate to Settings: Open Claude Desktop or claude.ai, click on your profile icon, and select "Settings" → "Connectors"
Add Custom Connector: Scroll to the bottom and click "Add custom connector"
Enter Server URL: Input the TogoMCP server URL:
https://togomcp.rdfportal.org/mcp
Configure Authentication: TogoMCP uses Streamable HTTP transport and does not require OAuth authentication. Leave authentication settings at default.
Activate Connector: Click "Add" to finish configuration. You can now enable TogoMCP tools via the "Search and tools" button in your chat interface.

Note: Claude Desktop also supports remote connectors on iOS and Android mobile apps. Once configured on claude.ai, the connector will be available on mobile devices.

ChatGPT (Developer Mode)

ChatGPT Business, Enterprise, and Education plans can connect to MCP servers using Developer Mode, which provides full MCP support including write actions.

Enable Developer Mode: Navigate to Settings → Apps & Connectors → Advanced settings, and toggle Developer Mode on (requires admin/owner permissions)
Create Connector: In Settings → Connectors, click "Create"
Configure Server Details:
  • Name: TogoMCP
  • Description: Life sciences database integration via MCP
  • Connector URL: https://togomcp.rdfportal.org/mcp
  • Authentication: Leave as default (no OAuth required)
Verify Connection: Click "Create" and verify that TogoMCP tools appear in the available tools list
Use in Chat: Start a new conversation, enable Developer Mode from the model selector, and select TogoMCP from available connectors

Important: Developer Mode shows confirmation dialogs for tool calls. Review these carefully, especially for tools that might query or modify data.

Gemini CLI

The Gemini CLI supports MCP servers through Streamable HTTP transport. Note: TogoMCP uses Streamable HTTP, not SSE.

Install Gemini CLI: Follow the installation instructions at geminicli.com
Add TogoMCP Server: Use the following command to configure TogoMCP: 
gemini mcp add --transport http togomcp https://togomcp.rdfportal.org/mcp
Optional Configuration: Add custom headers if needed: 
gemini mcp add --transport http \
  --header "Custom-Header: value" \
  togomcp https://togomcp.rdfportal.org/mcp
Verify Installation: Check that TogoMCP is loaded by running: 
/mcp
You should see TogoMCP tools listed among available tools.
Test the Connection: Try a simple query like "List available databases in TogoMCP" to verify the connection is working

Important: TogoMCP uses Streamable HTTP transport, not SSE. Always use --transport http when adding the server. Using SSE transport will result in connection errors.

Available Databases

TogoMCP provides access to over 20 major biological and biomedical databases, covering diverse categories from proteins and genes to chemical compounds, diseases, and clinical data:

uniprot
UniProt RDF
Comprehensive protein sequence and functional information with 444M proteins. Contains Swiss-Prot (manually curated, 923K entries) and TrEMBL (automatically annotated). Includes sequences, functions, domains, structures, variants, and cross-references to 200+ databases.
pubchem
PubChem RDF
Public database of chemical molecules with 119M compounds, 339M substances, 1.7M bioassays, 167K genes, 249K proteins, and 81K pathways. Provides molecular descriptors (SMILES, InChI, properties) and integrates chemical ontologies.
pdb
Protein Data Bank
3D structural data for proteins, nucleic acids, and complexes from X-ray, NMR, and cryo-EM with 204K+ entries. Contains experimental methods, resolution, R-factors, sequences, and cross-references to UniProt and EMDB.
chembl
ChEMBL RDF
Manually curated database of bioactive molecules with drug-like properties. Contains 2.4M+ compounds, 1.6M assays, 20M bioactivity measurements. Enables queries for compound-target-activity relationships and drug repositioning.
chebi
Chemical Entities of Biological Interest
Ontology database containing 217,000+ chemical entities including small molecules, atoms, ions, functional groups, and macromolecules with hierarchical classification. Provides molecular data and biological roles.
reactome
Reactome Pathway Database
Open-source, curated knowledgebase of biological pathways. Contains 22,000+ pathways across 30+ species with molecular interactions, biochemical reactions, protein complexes, and disease associations based on BioPAX Level 3 ontology.
rhea
Rhea - Annotated Reactions Database
Expert-curated database of biochemical reactions with 17,078 reactions, 11,763 small molecules, and 254 polymer structures. All reactions are atom-balanced and chemically annotated. Serves as the reference reaction resource for UniProtKB enzyme annotation.
mesh
Medical Subject Headings
National Library of Medicine's controlled vocabulary thesaurus for biomedical literature indexing. Hierarchical structure with descriptors, qualifiers, and supplementary chemical/disease records across 16 main categories.
go
Gene Ontology
Controlled vocabulary for describing gene and gene product attributes across all organisms. Organized into three domains: biological_process (30,804 terms), molecular_function (12,793 terms), and cellular_component (4,568 terms).
taxonomy
NCBI Taxonomy RDF
Comprehensive biological taxonomic classification covering 3M+ organisms from bacteria to mammals with hierarchical relationships, scientific/common names, and genetic code assignments. Essential for biological data integration.
mondo
Monarch Disease Ontology
Comprehensive disease ontology integrating multiple disease databases into unified classification. Provides cross-references to OMIM, Orphanet, DOID, MESH, ICD, and 35+ databases for clinical research and precision medicine.
nando
NANDO (Nanbyo Data)
Comprehensive ontology for Japanese intractable (rare) diseases with 2,777 disease classes. Includes multilingual labels (English, Japanese), descriptions, notification numbers, and cross-references to MONDO and KEGG.
ensembl
Ensembl RDF
Comprehensive genomics database providing genome annotations for 100+ species. Contains genes, transcripts, proteins, and exons with genomic locations. Cross-referenced to UniProt, HGNC, NCBI Gene, Reactome, and OMIM.
ncbigene
NCBI Gene Database
Gene database with 57M+ entries covering protein-coding genes, ncRNAs, pseudogenes across all organisms. Includes gene symbols, descriptions, chromosomal locations, types, synonyms, and cross-references to Ensembl, HGNC, OMIM.
pubmed
PubMed
Bibliographic information for biomedical literature from MEDLINE, life science journals, and online books. Includes publication metadata (titles, abstracts, authors, affiliations, journals), MeSH term annotations, and cross-references.
pubtator
PubTator Central RDF
Biomedical entity annotations extracted from PubMed literature using advanced text mining. Contains Disease and Gene annotations linked to PubMed articles, enabling literature-based biomedical discovery and knowledge graph construction.
clinvar
ClinVar RDF
Aggregates genomic variation and its relationship to human health with 3.5M+ variant records, clinical interpretations, gene associations, and disease conditions. Cross-referenced to MedGen, OMIM, MeSH, and HGNC.
medgen
Medical Genetics
NCBI's portal for medical conditions with genetic components containing 233,000+ clinical concepts covering diseases, phenotypes, and clinical findings. Integrates data from OMIM, Orphanet, Human Phenotype Ontology (HPO), and MONDO.
glycosmos
GlyCosmos RDF Database
Comprehensive glycoscience portal integrating glycan structures (GlyTouCan), glycoproteins, glycosylation sites, glycogenes, glycoepitopes, and lectin-glycan interactions across 100+ named graphs for multi-species glycobiology research.
bacdive
BacDive - Bacterial Diversity Metadatabase
Standardized bacterial and archaeal strain information covering taxonomy, morphology, physiology, cultivation conditions, and molecular data. Contains 97,000+ strain records with phenotypic and genotypic characterizations.
mediadive
MediaDive - Microbial Culture Media Database
Comprehensive culture media database from DSMZ with 3,289 standardized recipes for bacteria, archaea, fungi, yeast, microalgae, and phages. Includes 1,489 ingredients with chemical cross-references and 45,685 strain records.
ddbj
DNA Data Bank of Japan
Nucleotide sequence data from the International Nucleotide Sequence Database Collaboration. Contains entries with genomic annotations (genes, CDS, tRNA, rRNA) linked to organism metadata, taxonomic classification, and functional annotations.

Available Tools

TogoMCP provides a comprehensive suite of tools for accessing and integrating life sciences data:

list_databases
List all available databases with their descriptions and key characteristics
get_sparql_endpoints
Get the available SPARQL endpoints for each RDF database
get_MIE_file
Get the MIE file containing ShEx schema, RDF and SPARQL examples for a specific database. Essential for constructing queries
get_sparql_example
Get example SPARQL queries for a specific RDF database to understand query patterns
run_sparql
Execute SPARQL queries on a specific RDF database to retrieve and analyze data
get_graph_list
Get a list of named graphs available in a specific RDF database
search_uniprot_entity
Search for UniProt protein entity IDs by query terms
search_pdb_entity
Search for PDBj entry information by keywords in PDB, Chemical Component, or BIRD databases
search_chembl_molecule
search_chembl_target
search_chembl_id_lookup
Search for ChEMBL molecules, targets, and perform ID lookups for bioactivity data
search_mesh_entity
Search for MeSH (Medical Subject Headings) IDs by query terms
get_pubchem_compound_id
get_compound_attributes_from_pubchem
Get PubChem compound IDs by name and retrieve compound attributes including chemical properties
togoid_getAllDataset
togoid_getDataset
Get configuration for all or specific TogoID datasets with labels, regex patterns, prefixes, and examples
togoid_getAllRelation
togoid_getRelation
Get all possible conversion relationships or specific relationships between databases
togoid_convertId
Convert IDs from one database to another with support for multi-step conversions
togoid_countId
Count how many IDs can be converted between databases
togoid_getDescription
Get descriptions for all databases in English and Japanese with organization information
RDF_Portal_Guide
General guideline for using the RDF Portal and understanding its capabilities

Related Resources

TogoMCP is part of a broader ecosystem of tools and services developed by DBCLS for life sciences data integration:

TogoMCP Server MCP Endpoint

The production MCP server endpoint providing access to all TogoMCP tools and databases. This is the URL you configure in your AI assistant to connect to TogoMCP's capabilities.

RDF Portal Data Repository

The NBDC RDF Portal is a comprehensive repository for semantic data in life sciences, hosting over 20 RDF datasets with more than 45.5 billion triples. All datasets are reviewed by NBDC to ensure interoperability and queryability. The portal provides SPARQL endpoints, faceted search interfaces, and downloadable datasets following DBCLS guidelines for RDFizing databases. It serves as the underlying infrastructure that powers TogoMCP's database access capabilities.

TogoID ID Conversion

TogoID is an identifier conversion service that links IDs across 65+ life science databases covering diverse categories including genes, proteins, compounds, diseases, and genetic polymorphisms. Unlike traditional ID converters, TogoID supports cross-category conversions (e.g., from diseases to related genes) and multi-step conversions through intermediate databases. Features include a user-friendly web interface, comprehensive API for programmatic access, semantic representation of ID relationships, and weekly automatic data updates. TogoID powers the ID conversion capabilities integrated into TogoMCP.

PubDictionaries MCP Server Complementary Tool

PubDictionaries is an MCP server that provides biomedical term annotation and dictionary services. It complements TogoMCP by offering tools to find identifiers by term labels, look up terms from identifiers, and search across multiple life science dictionaries. When used together with TogoMCP, PubDictionaries enables seamless translation between human-readable terms and database identifiers, enhancing the natural language capabilities of AI assistants in biomedical contexts.

DBCLS (Database Center for Life Science) Developer

The Database Center for Life Science (DBCLS) is a Japanese research institute and part of the Research Organization of Information and Systems (ROIS), founded in 2007. DBCLS conducts broad-ranging research from fundamental technology development for database integration using Semantic Web technology to the development of web services and tools that enhance the usability of life sciences databases. DBCLS has been organizing the annual BioHackathon series since 2008 (co-organizing with NBDC since 2011), which aims to develop technologies and resources that improve integration, preservation, and utilization of databases in life sciences. DBCLS develops and maintains TogoMCP, TogoID, RDF Portal, and numerous other bioinformatics resources.
